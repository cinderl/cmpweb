### Intrinsic Value Calculations

**1. Dividend Discount Model (DDM)**
The DDM is the most relevant model for a dividend-focused asset manager like TROW.
* $D_1$ (Expected Dividend for 2026) = $5.08 per share (Based on a projected $1.27 quarterly run-rate).
* $r$ (Required Rate of Return / Cost of Equity) = $9.5\%$ (A reasonable rate for a low-debt, blue-chip financial company).
* $g$ (Expected Long-Term Dividend Growth Rate) = $5.0\%$ (A conservative assumption, below the historical 5-year CAGR of 8.98%).

$$\text{Intrinsic Value} = \frac{D_1}{r - g} = \frac{\$5.08}{0.095 - 0.050} = \frac{\$5.08}{0.045} = \mathbf{\$112.89}$$

**2. Asset-based Valuation (2024 YE)**
This method is less indicative of fair value for service-based businesses like TROW, which generate value from intangible assets (client contracts, brand, expertise) not fully reflected on the balance sheet.
* Total Assets (2024) = $13.47 Billion
* Total Liabilities (2024) = $2.02 Billion
* Intrinsic Value (Equity) = Total Assets - Total Liabilities = $11.45 Billion
* Intrinsic Value per Share = $11.45 Billion / 223 Million shares $\approx \mathbf{\$51.35}$

**Conclusion:** We rely on the **DDM Intrinsic Value of \$112.89**.

### Target Buy Price

Applying a 20% to 30% Margin of Safety (MOS) to the DDM Value:
